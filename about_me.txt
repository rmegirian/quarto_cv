## Senior-leaning version

Working at the intersection of complexity science, human behaviour, research evidence, and design, I specialise in understanding and shaping change within complex systems. My work focuses on identifying what is driving current outcomes, where leverage for change exists, and how strategy, interventions, and communication can be aligned with the realities of human behaviour and organisational context.

Drawing on expertise in cognition, psychology, research methodology, and design, I integrate diverse forms of evidence to support clear strategic direction in situations characterised by uncertainty or competing priorities. Across roles spanning research, policy, community development, program design, and stakeholder engagement, I have contributed to initiatives ranging from field-based research and community programs to organisational strategy, consistently connecting insight, people, and context to enable meaningful and sustainable change.

Fluent in qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods approaches, I bring the capacity to shape methodological direction and interpret complex evidence in ways that inform robust decision-making, while remaining attentive to the relational and contextual dynamics that determine whether change succeeds in practice. Integrating systems thinking with human-centred design, I work across disciplinary and organisational boundaries to support outcomes that are effective, adaptive, and responsive to complex environments.

## Earlier Version

Working at the intersection of complexity science, human behaviour, research evidence, and design, I combine methodological rigour with human-centred insight to understand, design, and fascilitate change in complex systems.

Grounded in a deep understanding of cognition, psychology, research methodology, design, and the complex, interacting systems and contexts that shape behaviour and outcomes, I focus on understanding what is driving current conditions and where meaningful change is possible. This enables me to identify leverage points, generate and communicate relevant insights, and help teams make sense of complexity while shaping strategy and communication around shared objectives. Decisions are therefore informed not only by evidence, but also by how people interpret, experience, and respond to change in practice.

Applied across roles spanning research, policy, community development, program design, and stakeholder engagement in public, private, and not-for-profit contexts, this approach has supported a wide range of initiatives — from evidence-informed research and field-based projects to community programs and organisational strategy. While the settings and goals have varied, the underlying focus has remained consistent: connecting insight, people, and context to co-design interventions, processes, and communications that achieve meaningful change.

Fluent in qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods approaches, I am able to guide methodological choices and interpret insights to ensure strategy is informed by robust, reliable evidence while simultaneously attending to the relational and contextual dynamics that determine how change is understood and adopted in practice. My work aligns organisational goals with the realities of human behaviour, stakeholder relationships, and operational environments. By integrating systems thinking with human-centred design, I support organisations and communities to achieve outcomes that are effective, sustainable, and responsive to the complex systems in which they operate.
